Cash/Credit Funds

A cash fund is a managed investment scheme that aims to provide competitive returns investing in low-risk, high liquidity cash or cash equivalents such as short-term government bonds or bank bills. The underlying component of a credit fund is that the fund is taking a view on exposure to certain counterparties’ credit. Schemes can be registered or unregistered.

Equity Funds

Invest primarily in equities or companies which can be listed or unlisted. Equity funds can have a focus on sector, geography or market capitalisation or can be diversified. Managed funds, also known as unit trusts, allow investors to pool their money with others, thus enabling them to invest in assets and markets that may be difficult to access individually.

Funds of Funds

A fund of funds, also referred to as a multi-manager investment, deploys capital across a portfolio of underlying funds rather than investing directly into assets such as equities, bonds, infrastructure or real estate. More simply, a fund of funds is any fund that pools capital and utilises the expertise of two or more sub managers to invest that capital.

Property Funds

A managed fund that invests in real estate assets that may otherwise be too expensive for investors to invest in directly. The investment strategy is typically yield, capital growth, development or a combination of these. Investments can be made in a single property or a portfolio of properties and can be commercial, retail industrial or residential real estate.

Income Funds

An income fund is a managed investment scheme that seeks to generate an income stream by investing into assets that pay dividends or make interest payments, rather than generate capital appreciation. The funds can be invested into a broad range of investments including bonds, equities, hybrids, cash or even real estate investment Trusts (REITs).

Infrastructure Funds

An infrastructure fund is an investment scheme that invests primarily in facilities and utilities that provide essential services to communities. These characteristics generally result in less volatile earnings. Assets include airports, toll roads, seaports, railways, hospitals, education facilities, water supply, gas and electricity & telecommunications facilities.

Private Equity Funds

Private equity funds are managed investment schemes that normally invest in private, non-publicly traded companies and businesses. Typically, the fund will be structured as a unit trust or limited partnership that raises capital from institutional and sophisticated investors and sometimes retail investors, which is used to buy equity interests in companies.

Social Impact Funds

Managed investment schemes that make investments that create positive and measurable social or environmental change alongside appropriate financial returns. Investments can be made into companies, organisations or funds, whether they be not-for-profit or for-profit. Investments in this space can include property, infrastructure, corporations or private equity.
